# Tips before going live

You've set everything up. Before you flip the switch, run through these five checks to make sure your customers get a smooth experience from day one.

---

## 1. Make a test booking

Book one of your own boats as if you were a customer. Open your booking form, pick a date, select a boat, fill in your details, and complete the payment.

Then check both sides:

- **As a renter:** Did you get a confirmation email? Is the information correct? Does it look professional?
- **As an operator:** Does the booking show up in your dashboard? Are all the details there?

This is the single most valuable thing you can do before going live.

---

## 2. Try your booking form

Click through your booking form and verify everything works the way you want it to.

- Is the pricing calculated correctly for different durations?
- Do your photos and descriptions look right?
- Are [add-ons](/guides/settings/create-add-ons) showing up where they should?
- Can you complete a booking without getting stuck?

If something feels off, tweak your [styling](https://dashboard.letsbook.app/styling) or [booking form settings](https://dashboard.letsbook.app/booking-form).

---

## 3. Log in as a customer

When you make a test booking, create a customer account during the process. Then log in as that customer and see what they see.

- Can they view their bookings?
- Can they modify or cancel if you allow that?
- Is the right information visible (and nothing more)?

---

## 4. Verify test payments

> [Stripe dashboard](https://dashboard.stripe.com/test/payments) or [Mollie dashboard](https://my.mollie.com)

Check that your test payments actually arrived in your payment provider. Look for:

- The correct amounts
- The right descriptions on each transaction
- Successful payment confirmations

This is also a good time to test a failed payment scenario. Try using a declined test card to make sure customers get a clear error message.

---

## 5. Review your notifications

> [Configure notifications](https://dashboard.letsbook.app/notifications)

Go through the emails your test booking generated. Check:

- Booking confirmation
- Payment confirmation
- Any reminder emails
- Cancellation and modification messages

Read them as if you were a customer. Are they clear? Do they contain the right info? Is your company name and contact info correct?

---

## Good to know about test bookings

- **Your own test bookings stay.** Any bookings you created yourself remain in the system after going live. You can delete them manually if you want a clean start.
- **Let's Book test bookings disappear.** Bookings generated by the Let's Book team (marked with a test label) are automatically removed when your account goes live.

---

## More test scenarios to consider

If you want to be extra thorough:

- Test booking modifications and cancellations
- Try different pricing scenarios (weekday vs. weekend, multi-day, discounts)
- Verify [blockout dates](/guides/settings/rental-setups/schedules) are actually blocked
- Have a friend go through the booking flow and give honest feedback

Use this testing phase to fine-tune your availability, pricing, or messaging based on what you find.

---

## Invite your team

> [Invite team members](https://dashboard.letsbook.app/team)

If other people will be managing bookings, invite them before you go live. You can assign roles like Operations Manager, Planner, or Dock Host to control what each person can access.

Read more: [Invite team members](/guides/settings/collaborate/invite-team-members) · [Roles and permissions](/guides/settings/collaborate/roles-permissions)

---

## Ready? Go live!

Once everything checks out:

1. [Add billing information](https://dashboard.letsbook.app/account/billing) for your Let's Book account
2. Publish your setup to make it live
3. [Add the booking form to your website](/guides/settings/booking-form/add-to-website)
